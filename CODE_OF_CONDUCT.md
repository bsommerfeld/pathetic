# Code of Conduct 🥀

**Pathetic, the library, is allowed to trash-talk. You are not.**

We write a README that calls competing libraries cowards and benchmarks that humiliate hardware. That is the *library's* personality — not a license for community members to be cruel to each other. Around here, the only thing we drag is `O(n²)`.

> "Be Pathetic in your code. Don't be pathetic to people." <br>
> — the entire point of this document

---

## Our Pledge

We — members, contributors, and maintainers — pledge to make participation in the Pathetic community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community. Yes, even when someone submits a PR that allocates inside the hot loop.

---

## Our Standards

### Things that make this community great

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback ("this is slow because X" — yes; "you're slow because X" — no)
- Owning your mistakes, apologizing, and learning from them
- Putting the health of the community above your own ego — even when your benchmark numbers are objectively better

### Things that get you removed from the food chain

- Sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information (physical address, email, anything you wouldn't want published about yourself) without their explicit permission
- Any other conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

The README brags. People don't. Know the difference.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. We will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior we deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will explain moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all Pathetic community spaces — GitHub (issues, PRs, discussions), Discord, and anywhere else this project lives. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces (official email, social media account, or appointed representative at an event, online or offline).

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the maintainers via the community Discord: **https://discord.gg/HMqCbdQjX9**.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

We take reports seriously. We do not take retaliation against reporters lightly — that itself is a violation.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines when determining consequences:

| Level                | What triggered it                                                                                                         | What happens                                                                                                                                                                              |
|----------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1. Correction**    | Inappropriate language or behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.                                                    | Private written warning from maintainers with clarity around the nature of the violation and why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.                       |
| **2. Warning**       | A violation through a single incident or series of actions.                                                               | Warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved — including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the CoC — for a specified period.       |
| **3. Temporary Ban** | A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.                                   | Temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved during this time.        |
| **4. Permanent Ban** | A pattern of violation, sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward a class of individuals. | Permanent ban from any public interaction within the community. Don't come back.                                                                                                  |
